Randolph House

Randolph is a nine bedroom house in Champaign focused on gardening, cooking, and cooperative living. The house previously was operated as the Catholic Worker House and served the homeless population of Champaign, and we aim to honor this history by offering accessible and affordable housing, as well as sharing our food and produce with the community. Randolph is currently leased from Jonah Weisskopf, unlike Brooks and Harvest which are owned by NASCO Properties, which mostly just means house members are not responsible for as much maintenance.

Randolph House

Randolph House is a nine bedroom co-op situated in Champaign. The house is a short walk from downtown Champaign and all the entertainment and cultural resources the city center offers!

About the House

Bedrooms

Randolph is a three-story house with a furnished basement, with bedroms in the basement and first and second floor. There are five bathrooms in the house! All bedrooms are completely furnished with a bed, dresser and desk.
The average rent per room is $295 per month, with rates ranging from $275/month to $370/month. Utilities and food together are around another $200 per month.

Food

We prepare home-cooked, healthy, vegetarian and vegan-friendly dinners five nights a week. Each person cooks around once a week, so one or two people make each meal. We buy food in bulk and grow lots of produce during the summer. We strive to buy local when we can and organic when it is the most sustainable option.

Amenities

Common spaces include a large kitchen with industrial-quality appliances, two ovens, flat top, large range hood, pantry, dining room, living room, laundry room, a huge porch, and the entire third floor is common area for crafts, yoga, jam sessions, or whatever floats your boat.

We have Ethernet internet connections available and high-speed wifi.

We also have a washer and dryer, bike rack and compost bins all of which are free to use for housemates.

Labor and Decision Making

Randolph upholds pretty standard operating procedures for a co-op. Check out the FAQs for more details. We make decisions that affect the house either through email (in urgent or low-stakes cases) or at our weekly house meetings.

House meetings are usually at 7pm on Mondays, depending on what is most convenient for members.

Members

Randolph is open to anyone regardless of occupation or student status! The house is usually a mixture of community members and students. House members come from all corners of the world and encompass a variety of cultural backgrounds.

Randolph is committed to diversity and accessibility.
